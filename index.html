What is the Cause of Police Brutality?

Here are seven reasons why police misconduct is a systematic problem, not "a few bad apples."

  1. Many departments do not provide adequate training and nonviolent solutions.
  2. Standards for what constitutes brutality vary widely.
  3. Consequences for misconduct are minimal.
  4. Those officers who are found guilty of brutality typically find the settlement to their victims paid from city coffers.
  5. Minorities are unfairly targeted through racism.
  6. Police are increasingly militarized.
  7. Police themselves say misconduct is remarkably widespread.

Where Police Brutality Happens and who is Targetted?

Police brutality mainly happens in locations with a high population of minorities because they are usually the ones that are affected by police brutality.
